# How to File a Claim

In most cases, if you visit an in-network provider, you don't need to file a claim yourself — the provider bills Harborview Health Insurance directly. You only need to file a claim manually in certain situations.

## When you need to file a claim yourself
- You saw an out-of-network provider who doesn't bill us directly
- You received care while traveling outside your home region
- You paid out of pocket and need reimbursement
- You used emergency care at a facility that wasn't able to verify your coverage at the time

## Steps to file a claim
1. Download the claim form from the member portal or request one from member services
2. Attach an itemized receipt or bill from the provider, including the date of service, procedure codes, and amount paid
3. Mail the completed form and receipts to the claims department address printed on the form, or upload them through the member portal
4. Claims are typically processed within 14 business days of receipt

## Explanation of Benefits (EOB)
After a claim is processed, you'll receive an Explanation of Benefits. This is not a bill — it shows what was billed, what Harborview paid, and what (if anything) you owe the provider directly.

## If a claim is denied
You have the right to appeal any denied claim within 180 days of the denial notice. To start an appeal:
1. Call member services or submit an appeal request through the member portal
2. Include any supporting documentation, such as a letter from your doctor explaining medical necessity
3. Harborview will respond to standard appeals within 30 days, and expedited appeals (for urgent medical situations) within 72 hours

## In-network vs. out-of-network
Using in-network providers generally costs less because Harborview has negotiated rates with them. Out-of-network care is covered on most plans but at a lower percentage, and you may be billed for the difference between what the provider charges and what Harborview considers a reasonable rate (this is called "balance billing").
