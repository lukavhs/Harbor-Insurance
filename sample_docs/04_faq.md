# Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I find an in-network doctor?**
Use the "Find a Provider" tool on the member portal, or call member services at the number on the back of your insurance card. Always confirm with the provider's office that they're still in-network before your visit, since networks can change.

**How do I add a spouse or child to my plan?**
You can add a dependent within 30 days of a qualifying life event (marriage, birth, adoption, loss of other coverage) by logging into the member portal and selecting "Add Dependent," or by calling member services. Outside of a qualifying event, dependents can only be added during the annual open enrollment period.

**What happens to my coverage if I lose my job?**
You may be eligible for COBRA continuation coverage, which lets you keep your same plan temporarily at full cost (including the portion your employer previously paid). You may also qualify for a special enrollment period to switch to an individual marketplace plan within 60 days of losing job-based coverage.

**Can I keep my doctor if I switch plans?**
Only if that doctor is in-network for your new plan. Check the provider directory for your new plan tier before switching, since not all providers participate in every tier.

**Do I need a referral to see a specialist?**
No. Harborview Health Insurance plans do not require referrals to see in-network specialists. However, some specialist services (such as elective surgery) still require prior authorization.

**How do I get a replacement insurance card?**
Request one through the member portal under "My Profile," or call member services. A digital copy of your card is also always available in the mobile app.

**What's the difference between a copay and coinsurance?**
A copay is a fixed dollar amount you pay for a service (like $30 for a doctor visit). Coinsurance is a percentage of the cost you pay after your deductible is met (like 20% of a hospital bill). See the glossary document for more detail.

**Is dental or vision included?**
No, dental and vision are sold as separate add-on plans and are not included in the Bronze, Silver, Gold, or Platinum medical plans.
