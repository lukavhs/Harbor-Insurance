# Glossary of Insurance Terms

**Premium** — The amount you pay each month to keep your insurance plan active, regardless of whether you use any services.

**Deductible** — The amount you must pay out of pocket for covered services before your insurance plan starts sharing the cost. Preventive care is typically exempt from the deductible.

**Copay (copayment)** — A fixed dollar amount you pay for a specific service, such as $30 for a primary care visit, regardless of the total cost of that service.

**Coinsurance** — Your share of costs for a covered service, calculated as a percentage, after you've met your deductible. For example, 20% coinsurance means you pay 20% of the allowed amount and your plan pays the remaining 80%.

**Out-of-pocket maximum** — The most you'll have to pay for covered services in a plan year. Once you reach this amount, Harborview pays 100% of covered services for the rest of the year. Premiums do not count toward this maximum.

**Network** — The group of doctors, hospitals, and other providers that have agreed to negotiated rates with Harborview Health Insurance. Visiting in-network providers generally costs less.

**Prior authorization** — Approval from Harborview that is required before certain services, procedures, or medications will be covered. Getting care without prior authorization when it's required can result in a denied claim.

**Explanation of Benefits (EOB)** — A statement sent after a claim is processed showing what was billed, what the plan paid, and what you may owe. It is not a bill.

**Balance billing** — When an out-of-network provider bills you for the difference between their charge and the amount your insurance plan considers reasonable.

**Formulary** — The list of prescription drugs covered by your plan, organized into cost tiers.
